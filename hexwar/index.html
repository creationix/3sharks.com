Castle Siege
Hexwar: Castle Seige is another installment in the Hexwar World in the genre of Midieval European feudal system. Just as flexible and expandable as the original, Castle Siege allows for more of a storyline witht eh complexities of diplomacy getting deeper as you deal with defending you lands from barbarians as well as you neighboring lands. Set up a castle, and towers for defence, try your might, as your evil neighbors eye your crops and land. Can you hold back the forces of a barrage of catapult boulders hurl at your west wall, and Barbarians picking away at your defences at your east? This game expands the concept of theme strategy gaming into knights, archers, barbarians. GOT MOAT?

DEFINITIONS:
HEX = a single six-sided "space" on the map.
HP = Hit Point, the points of "health" a unit has remaining. (Noted by circles on the Manifest)
UNIT = a single playing piece to be used on the map.
RESOURCES = Un-purchased units or supplies allowed to you for the game.
CASTLE = Headquarters, the royal household for your mighty laws and decree.
CATAPULT = Main Siege weapon of Medieval Europe. Destroys Defences by hurling stones.
ARCHER = Foot soldier with Bows and Arrows
PIKEMAN = Basic Defence.
KNIGHT = Soldier on Horseback, moves fast, and kills quickly. Champion of your lands.
KING = You, or any other player of the game.

Manifest = The sheet that you record information of all of your playing pieces. (Unit Number, HP...)


DICE INSTRUCTIONS:
*For each game you must have 6-6sided dice (Or a 6-D6 number generator on computer.).*
When attacking, or defending, roll the number of dice equal to the attacking units' attack score.
Rolling an even number on one represents a "success". (Each Die has a 2/3 chance of a success.)
(Attacker Successes - Defender Successes) = HITS (The number of HPs lost by defender.)

BATTLE EXAMPLE- A Knight (4 attack dice) attacks an Archer unit (2 Defend Dice).
  • A Knight's King rolls 4 dice and scores 2, 2, 6, 5 (3 successes  - since "evens" are counted as hits.)
  • The Enemy Archer's King rolls 2 dice and scores 6,5 (0 successes - since "odds" are misses.)
  • Knight 3 vs. Archer 1  = +2 (The Knight hits the Archer unit and causes 2 HP of damage destroying those Archers.).
DAMAGE RULES:
  • If the attacker rolls more successes than the defender, the difference between the two is the amount of HP lost by the defender.(If the Archer Unit rolled a 2, 4 (See above) it would have taken 0 points of damage, Knight misses.)
  • If you have more than one unit on a single HEX, the attacker chooses a target within that HEX..

    • HPs of a unit can be restored, but must make it into It's King's
LOSING PIECES:
    When a unit loses all HPs, it is removed from the board and held by the destroyer of that unit.
     This is used to show overall damage at the end of the game.


Each player starts by selecting their color (color of paper units are printed on),
and setting up their Castle on the map.

SETUP:
Each Player starts with:

  • One Castle and one Tower
  • Three PIKEMAN units
  • One ARCHER unit.
  • 20 GP
Place Castle on Map. The center hex of the Castle is the starting location of ALL new pieces.

TURN:
A players turn consists of these steps in this order:
  • Place Units - Must start in CASTLE, Record the unit number, and stats of each unit on your Manifest sheet.

    • Leave open 1 dot per HP for that unit... fill in the rest.
  • One move and One action per Unit.

    • (Movement rates can be divided into two parts - part before and part after attack.)
  • Hire new units (To be placed in CASTLE on your NEXT turn.)
  • TAX your kingdom (collect 5 GP for each occupied TOWER you own), declare end of turn.

    • NOTE: Collecting TAX is always the LAST action taken for a turn..
TAXING LANDS:
  • TAX = 5 GP + (5GP / OCCUPIED TOWER.) - (A TOWER is occupied when a unit you control is stationed in it.)
