How do floods on the Arkansas River effect the Conway area.

                                                                                                  

                The constant flooding of small rivers and streams cause little trouble compared to the amount of damage that a 100 year flood can cause.  A flood is a sudden and overwhelming surge of water flowing down a stream or river.  Flooding can cause familles to be lost and their belongings destroyed.  Every year thousands of acres are flooded in one of natures natural disasters.  In their own ways floods can be helpful and devastating.  Whole towns can be lost if levees and dams are not built properly and are not kept in good working order.  Floods also freshen up farm land by a depositing a layer of very fertile silt. 

                Major floods are classified into several different groups.  These groups classify each flood according to its intensity.  The groups are set up in percentages of the chance that the certain kind of flood will occur.  These are 2, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, and 500 year floods.  With a one hundred year flood there would be a 1 percent chance that a flood of this intensity would occur in the given area.  The worst flood would be a 500 year flood because it would have the highest crest level and the water would cover the most amount of land.  Floods of the two to ten year range occur the most often and are not very serious or damaging. These kind of floods rarely get very far out of the rivers immediate area.   The most recent major flood that occurred in the Faulkner county area was a 100 year flood which occurred in 1990.  Its crest level was approximately 56.6 feet instead of the normal 23 feet at Murray Lock and Dam in North Little Rock which is very close to conway.  A one hundred year flood would be one like the ones that occurred on the Mississippi River several years ago.  In these floods multiple houses were washed away, levees were broken, and hundreds of acres of farmland were covered with water and silt.  The last 500 year flood that occurred was in 1927(Kates 1985:209).  In this flood the North Little Rock business district was covered in three feet of water.  If a 500 year flood was to occur now and the Corp of Engineers were not very well prepared for the situation almost all of Conway would be under water and there would be catastrophic damage to all that was in the way of the water.  The landscape would be altered in great ways and the river might even change course. 

                When the latest one hundred year flood occurred, if  the levees would have broken much of the surrounding areas would have been flooded with several feet of water and mud. Some of the south part of the University of Central Arkansas’s campus would have been flooded.  The areas in and around the South Hampton subdivision are also in the flood plain.  Areas are considered to be in the flood plain if the areas elevation above sea level is below certain level for a given area.  Most areas are considered to be in the flood plain in the Conway region if the elevation is below about three hundred feet above sea level.  A majority of the area that UCA stands on is slightly higher than the flood plain.   

                Due to the vast amount of low bottomland in the surrounding areas there would be a great deal of farmland that would have been covered by a lot of sand, mud, and water.  When a big flood occurs it will usually leave a lot of sand and mud when the water recedes because of the heavy silt load that the river carries with it down stream.  The silt load of a stream is how much dirt and sand the water carries with it down stream.  The higher the silt load the larger amount of mud and sand a river carries and the more it will leave when the water recedes.  If a lot  of sand is deposited then it ruins the farmlands productivity.  The sand will not support any kind of crop because it dries out to quickly.  If a lot of good fertile dirt is deposited then the farmer will benefit from the flood in more ways than one.  The new dirt will produce higher yields from crops for several years.  Floods also can carry dirt away because we lose about “three million acres of land each year” to erosion caused by rivers and high water(Sampson 1978:13).  If there are a lot of houses are in the flood plain then the cost of the damage that a flood will cause will go up drastically along with the number of human casualties. 

                Allowing people to build houses in the flood plain is not the most reasonable thing that has been done.  The main reason why people build in the flood plain is because the land is usually cheaper and is flatter.  “In some areas the price of land in the flood plain is 50 percent cheaper than that of land not in the flood plain”(Presley).  A tract of land that is in the flood plain can be built on as long as a permit acquired and the structure is built at least one foot above ground level.  There is also what is called a “flood way, in this area no kind of dwelling can be built” because it is considered to be in the main channel of a flooded waterway(Nelessen 1994:116).  In areas where land is extremely cheap then the price reduction due to the land being in the flood plain is little to none, but when the land is part of  a subdivision such as Westin Estates, then the price will have a significant reduction because people get scared when they think that their house will get flooded and are less likely to pay a lot for the land.    

                A large majority of flood damage can be prevented by proper use of dams and flood gates.  In most cases the floods are caused because of a lot of rain or snow melt upstream of the area.  If the controllers of the dams would prepare for the big rush of water ahead of time and let out some of the water already in the upstream pools then the water that is on the way would be able to be controlled and kept inside the rivers banks.  With properly built levees constructed in strategic places the floods can be kept in moderation and controlled.  Several factors determine where a levee is constructed and how high it is.  If there is a hill right on the bank of the river then there is no reason to build one.  If the land is low bottomland and is relatively flat then the water does not have to get very high above the bank to be able to spread over great distances and cover a lot of ground and if there are houses then it will do extensive damage to the structures.  In areas that are low in elevation then a levee needs to be constructed to limit the flow of the flood waters.  There are several sections of the Arkansas River that have levees to protect the surrounding communities and farmlands.
