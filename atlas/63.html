Demographics and Natural Resources

 

            The question that we will be answering in this paper is:  What effects do demographics in Arkansas have on the depletion of water, and what can we do to prevent this problem?

            The first thing to consider when answering this question is demographics.  What are demographics?  Well, demography is the study of a population in terms of specific characteristics.  In other words, “Demography means (describing people)” (Bergman, Renwick 156).  In this paper we will consider the affects that people have on natural resources.

            What exactly are natural resources?  According to Edward Bergman and William Renwick, authors of Geography:  People, Places, and Environment, “A natural resource is anything created through natural processes” (326).  Examples of natural resources include:  plants, coal, land, metals, rock, and energy.  Natural resources are broken up into two categories:  nonrenewable and renewable.  Nonrenewable resources include those that cannot be replaced when depleted.  Nonrenewable resources include:  coal, gas, oil, and some metals.  Renewable resources include those that can be replenished.  Renewable resources include:  water, trees, air, wind, solar energy, land, etc.  Managing these resources can be a major problem when faced with a growing population and demand.

Matt Scroggin-What is the demographic problem in Arkansas?

This is the most important resource we as Arkansans have for protection.  If we do not, there are several consequences we will face.  We can count on dealing with various problems such as loss of water for drinking, growing and producing.   This being what we as humans need to easily continue with our lives.  Suddenly when that is taken away, life is not so normal.  The consumption of water we use far exceeds our personal needs.  We use 86% of the ground water and 50% of surface water in Arkansas.  These aquifers that we use are being drained much faster than what naturally could be replaced.  Before we know it the tragedy will catch up with the human race. 

            These processes of water depletion are not moving at an extreme rate, they are moving gradually.  We can not tell very easily, how much ground water we have left or if the water has been contaminated.  For years the water has been clean and in abundance but times are changing.  When the faucet shuts off we will think twice about how much water we used today.  How far will we let our state's water deplete out of existence.  I do not think that people of Arkansas realize what will happen to us if we totally deplete out water supply.  When the water is gone it is gone.  It will not be back for a very long time. 

            We will lose our ability to supply our own water.  We will only have the rivers and that still would be a problem, not everyone lives near a river.  It would cause an economic fluctuation and the prices of all row crops will be higher due to the shortage of water which is the main part of it's production.  Families that live out of town will have to wait to get their water.  The situation would be disastrous because of all the fluctuations that will occur in the state's economy and agriculture.

Ground water is the water found in spaces between soil particles and rocks, and within cracks of the bedrock (Ground Water).  In the state of Arkansas there once was an abundance of ground water that has since been depleted as a result of the actions of our society.  Is the main reason for the depletion of our ground water a problem with demographics or is it the economic stress that has been put on the availability of the water?  The answer is that both are causes for the depletion, but the worldwide increases in population are the main reason for Arkansas’ ground water problem.  It is the products that we make and send out of the state combined with the effects of people.

Ground water is often taken for granted, but recent circumstances indicate that groundwater is seriously vulnerable to depletion and pollution.  Some of the causes of the depletion of the availability of natural water in Arkansas are a result of the increasing demographic problem.  The main reason is farming and the resulting increase is the demand for water.  The main reason for the depleting availability of water in Arkansas is the increase demand for the agricultural products, which Arkansas produces: rice, paper, and soybeans (mainly the first two) (Ground Water). (See attached table and first figure)

            Rice fields must remain flooded at all times in order to ensure proper growth of the plants.  They must get water which is drawn from the water table, see first attached figure.  There is only a certain amount of water that is added to the water table while on the other hand farmers take out too much water.  The other alternative is to channel water from a river or other body of surface water.  This causes negative effects as well, because the people downstream may not get enough water.  This is the greatest reason for the depletion of natural water in Arkansas.  To solve this shortage of water is a difficult thing to do.  We should combine the two problems that are created through the farming of rice and the production of paper.  Take the water that is contaminated through the paper mills and make it clean enough to use in the environment and then use it to flood the rice fields.  There are no books or suggestions that can be found with this idea presented as a solution, yet it is the most reasonable solution to the problems at hand.

            Extreme amounts of water are also required to produce paper.  In Arkansas, “The Natural State,” trees are grown and all different types of products are made as a result, the greatest being paper.  Large amounts of water are needed in order to get the paper to bond together and then the water becomes contaminated.  It must then be treated or the result is contaminated water that can no longer be used by people, see second attached figure.  Also when paper is produced there is another product produced; an enormous amount of water vapor is produced and the region looses all the water that is transformed into the water vapor.

            Every human activity has a particular effect on ground water.  Some agricultural activities contaminate the ground and thus all of the water that falls onto the ground becomes contaminated and unusable.  All vehicles leak some petroleum products, which also will contaminate ground water.  The most concentrated impact of water pollution comes from landfills.  Their contamination contains many different types of chemicals that will cause the water to be harmful to man and thus unusable (Causes). I do not know if we will ever be able to eliminate all the problems that mankind causes “Mother Nature,” but we may be able to limit them to a minimum.  Measures are being taken to make our actions as environment friendly as possible yet there will always remain consequences of every human activity.
