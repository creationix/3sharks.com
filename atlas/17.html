“Hughesies – where from and why here?”

            Cases)

Principles of Geography: Geog 1305, Section 2521

Prepared: November 19, 2000

 

Where do the residents of Hughes come from and why did they move to Hughes?

People move. For instance, people move from Paris, France to Paris, Arkansas.  This process of movement is known as migration.  Over time this process establishes certain unmistakable patterns. The students of the University of Central Arkansas (UCA) have all come here for a reason.  The residents of Hughes Residential College (HRC) have, too.  They come to UCA from places as close as one block away and as far as South Africa. If one examines where these residents of HRC are originally from, he or she can see that these students have established a migration stream to UCA.

In order to collect information for our statistics, we went from door-to-door handing out surveys (see survey #1 “Student Survey”).  We did this to see if we could find a migration pattern.  We asked for their birthplace (see chart #1 “Birthplace), Where they lived during high school, and where they graduated (see chart #2 “Where Are They From?”).  We also needed to figure out why people came to HRC.  Surveys were handed out to the hall coordinators, the Cossmans.  If we did not collect a survey from a person, we slid one under the door and instructed the person where to turn in the survey.  We had a 63% return rate.

Residents of Hughes come from all over the state.  There are people from out of state and even two from Africa.  The vast majority, though, is from Arkansas (see map #1 “Distribution Map of Population Coming to Hughes”).  Approximately 25-30% are from Little Rock and closely surrounding areas.  Only 2% of the people that stay in Hughes are from Conway, but that can be simply explained.  If a freshman has family in Conway,

he/ she does not have to stay in a dorm.  Otherwise, any freshmen must stay on campus.  There are fewer people from cities with other universities, for example, Fayetteville, Jonesboro, and Magnolia.  In fact, excluding Little Rock, only 5% of the HRC residents come from cities where there is another university.

The most common reason for coming to UCA is its location (see chart #3 “Reasons Why Hughes Residents Come to UCA”).  For most Hughesies, UCA is far enough away from home to get away from mom and dad, but close enough that they can still get home on the weekend if they want to.  Being only half an hour from Little Rock could also have been a factor for some of the Hughesies that are coming from farther away, that way they have all the advantages of a big city nearby without having to live there.  The next big reason for coming to UCA was scholarships (see the chart #4 “Hughesies’ Scholarships”).  Forty out of the 135 Hughesies who took our survey said that the scholarships offered by UCA were a primary factor in their coming here.  The third reason went along the same lines.  Thirty-three Hughesies said that the low cost of tuition and room and board was a factor in coming to UCA.  We decided to research this a little more and see how competitive UCA actually is.  We compared UCA with 12 other popular Arkansas Universities.  This survey was based on the annual cost of tuition and room and board.

            Many other students (20) based their decision partly on the appearance of the campus.  A few others came here because of alumni in the family, family wanted them to go, or friends were going to UCA.  A few put that they came here for academic programs and majors (see chart #5 “Majors”), the biggest being the Physical Therapy and Occupational Therapy programs. Finally, a few came here for the sports programs. 

            “Rather than view aggregate (amount) migration in terms of individual choices at a single point in time, it may be useful to view it as resulting from changes in either the location characteristics or the way migrants evaluate them” (Plane and Mulligan, 262).  The decision to migrate is affected by external and internal reasons.  With the student survey that was taken, we had an overview of the external and internal reasons why students live in this hall (see chart #6 “Reasons People Come to Hughes”).  One reason that students give for living in Hughes is that they were placed in HRC by chance.  This is external, because someone else, such as UCA Undergraduate Studies or the UCA Housing Department decided the outcome of where they were going to live.  More than 22 students stated that they were placed here by chance.  Another external reason that was listed for migration to Hughes was because of alumni in the family, family suggestions, and friends’ influence.  “Families are linked to potential places of destination through former household members or other relatives who have left the area.  Linkage to a system is represented by exchanges, such as information, potential social assistance, money and or emotional support between family and kin at the origin and potential places of destination” (Root and De Jong, 221).  Many students may have traveled here due to the fact that former family members in the past had lived in this dorm, and family members had advised it strongly.  Some families will, as this article suggests, advise their relatives to attend here, to a degree, that they will help in any way possible to see a trend continue.  A region’s spatial concentration may show how certain families’ in-out migration from their hometown to Hughes kept re-occurring through a series of years.  Atmosphere was tallied to be the biggest reason for students coming to Hughes.  That is an internal influence because of the fact that so many students see atmosphere in several different ways—from how they were brought up or how they have an outlook on life due to their personality.  Possibility net migration will occur in those areas that have experienced recent increase in attractiveness (Plane and Mulligan, 251).  Students are attracted to the programs that Hughes offers, and to the fact that it is a

co-educational hall.  Many students were attracted to Hughes by advertisements—either by word-of-mouth, fliers, or visitation to the campus.  This again goes back to the family and friends category.  Another reason that was listed on the survey was an ‘other’ category, in which case the students did not know, or understand, the external, or internal reasons that brought them here to Hughes.

            People come to UCA and Hughes from places as close as down the street to as far as Africa.  They come for a variety of reasons.  By examining our survey results, we were able to detect a migration pattern.  Approximately ¼ of the people come to Hughes from the Little Rock area.  We now know that Hughes residents come to UCA because of scholarships and location.  They come to HRC because of atmosphere and special programs offered in Hughes.  We found that our survey’s results reflected our own reasons for coming to Hughes.
