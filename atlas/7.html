How do different religions in Conway affect the communities social attitude and outbreak on life?

            This atlas assignment is composed mainly of attributes.  Attributes are characteristics or qualities ascribed to a person or thing (Oxford Desk Dictionary).  The type of attribute in this atlas is cultural.  Cultural attributes consist of the sense of conscious thinking and planning, transmission of skills and systems of social relationships, and creative modification of the environment (Microsoft Encarta 98).  The purpose of this atlas is to compare the different social affects that each religion has on the community members of Conway.  It will give a better understanding of each religion that we have identified in the Conway area and how each one plays a different role in the social ways of thinking by the community and University of Central Arkansas students.  A wide variety of churches of different religions exist in the Conway area.  By means of student surveys and church interviews we have found that each religion plays an important role in the decisions that each individual makes.

            Religion may intimately affect all factors of a culture.  Religious beliefs are by definition an element of the ideological subsystem; formalized and organized religion is an institutional expression of the sociological subsystem.  Religious beliefs strongly influence attitudes toward the tools and rewards of the technological subsystem (Introduction to Geography, p. 259). 

            All religions around the world have a certain way the people are to live their life in order to reach salvation.  These rules become interwoven with traditions of a culture.  Religion can also affect the economy.  Some religions will have certain crops to eat or specific things people are suppose to drink.  In some countries there is a state religion that intertwines religion and politics (Introduction to Geography p.259).  Religion varies in its cultural role- dominating in some societies, yet unimportant or rejected in others.  All societies have value systems- common beliefs, understandings, expectations, and controls- that unite their members and set them off from other different cultural groups (Introduction to Geography pp.262-265).

            Christianity had its origin in the life and teachings of Jesus whom his followers believed was the Messiah promised by God.  Christianity’s mission was conversion, as a universal religion of salvation.  They did this in hope that it would spread quickly among the underclasses of both the eastern and western parts of the Roman Empire.  The Christian faith was brought to the New World with European settlement.  Catholicism branched off of Christianity into the Orthodox Church, a descendent of the Byzantine State Church.  After a split in the church Roman Catholicism was supreme (Introduction to Geography p.262).

            Protestant Reformation of the 15th and 16th centuries split the church, installing a variety of Protestant denominations and national churches.  Protestantism placed less importance on the church as a monument and symbol (Introduction to Geography p.263).

            In Christian societies, the church assumes a prominent central position in the cultural landscape.  The cathedrals in all of Catholic Europe were located in the center of major cities.  Individually less imposing than the central cathedral of Catholic areas, the several Protestant churches common in small and large American towns collectively constitute an important land use, frequently sited in the center of the community.  Although religious intermingling rather than rigid territorial division is characteristic of the contemporary American scene, the beliefs and practices of various immigrant groups and the innovations of domestic congregations have created a particularly varied spatial patterning of “religious regions” in the United States (Introduction to Geography pp.262-265).

            In the Conway area we found an enormously high number of church’s, however, we only focused on a few religions.  We focused primarily on Assembly of God, Baptist, and Catholic, Church of Christ, Lutheran, Methodist, and Christian Religions.  We found information about each religion and contacted churches representative of each religion to answer a questionnaire.  We also had students at University of Central Arkansas answer a student survey that told: if they went to church in Conway, what religion and where their church was located, how long they attended, and a few other key questions.

            Assembly of God believes that Jesus Christ is the only Son of God and that he is the only way we can get to heaven (http://www.rsm4uth.org/).  First Assembly of God is a Christian Church located at Robinson and Mitchell streets, as seen on Map 1: church locations.  The First Assembly of God Church has been established since the 1920’s.  They have 200 regular members, 30 of these are University of Central Arkansas students.  In the church’s opinion it is critically important for students to be involved in a church while attending college.  They believe that college is a pivotal time and therefore it is important for students to have training to expand their knowledge of a greater power (Brian Stell, an employee of First Assembly of God).

            Baptist feel that it is their duty and privilege, as a follower of Christ, to strive to make disciples of all nations.  It is the role of every child of God to seek the will of God by personal effort and by all other methods in harmony with the gospel of Christ (http://www.sbcnet.org).  We interviewed the Bible Baptist Church and the Friendship Baptist Church both in the Conway area.  The Bible Baptist Church has been established for nine years.  They have 75 to 100 regular members that attend, and they have no University of Central Arkansas students that attend regularly.  The church does try to give them opportunities to participate, but the results have not been good so far (Billy Coulson, employee of Bible Baptist Church).  The Friendship Baptist Church has been established for 119 years.  They have 125 to 130 regular members that attend regularly and about three University of Central Arkansas students that attend.  They feel that the student involvement is very important because the focus and believing in the help they will need while attending college.  They feel that the students need a greater power to help them through their college years (Ron Richardson, employee of Friendship Baptist Church).

            The Roman Catholic Church realizes, proclaims, and celebrates the presence of Jesus Christ in their lives.  Roman Catholics are guided by the Holy Spirit to bring people to God and God to people (http://wpw.starnetinc.com).  The St. Joseph Catholic Church in Conway has been established since 1878 in Conway.  They feel that attending a church while in college is very important because it sets the ground level for the rest of your life through the decisions made.  They feel that even though they are located in a college town their main focus still remains on everyone, not just the college students (Mrs. Luneau employee of St. Joseph Catholic Church).

The Church of Christ does not claim to be a part of any denominational religious group.  People who attend do not claim to be members of the “ Church of Christ,” but instead members of the “Christ” which makes them members of the “Church.”  They have weekly worship services to remind them of God’s holiness and their sinfulness.  They believe that Christians are faced with constant attacks from Satan, and they must meet together to seek strength and encouragement from the attacks (http://www.cyberback.com).  We interviewed the University Church of Christ.  They have been established for 36 years in Conway.  They have 350 members that attend regularly and ten to 15 are University of Central Arkansas students.  They fell that it is very important for the students to be involved because they need support and strength during this time of their life (Lindyll Burlen, employee of University Church of Christ).

            The Advent Lutheran Church is committed to becoming a community of faith.  They desire to be much more attuned to “being the church rather that merely coming to church.”  The church is known for the way the members care for each other and serve the community in which they live. A growing emphasis at Advent is youth and family ministry (www.geocities.c).  The Advent Lutheran Church in Conway has been established for ten years.  They have 80 to 100 members that attend regularly.  They have four different outreach programs for students: youth, confirmation, high school, and young adult programs.  The roles of students are very important in the church.  They feel that it is very important that students are involved in a church while in college because it gives them an anchoring point for the issues that they face in college.  This way the students know that there is a place to go that has answers (Jo Hankins, employee of Advent Lutheran Church).

            The Methodist believe that it is their mission to make people Disciples of Christ.  They do this through different ways.  They proclaim the gospel, welcome and gather persons in the body of Christ.  They lead people to commit their lives to God through Jesus Christ, and they nurture people through worship, baptism, conformation, Bible, prayer, and other means of grace (http://www.umc.org).  We interviewed employees at  the Grace United Methodist Church and the First United Methodist Church.  The Grace United Methodist has been established for six years in Conway.  They have 340 one members that attend regularly and ten to 15 of them are University of Central Arkansas students.  They feel that student involvement is important because it gives them stability and a family away from their home (Tammy Garrison, employee of Grace United Methodist Church).  First United Methodist Church has been established for 102 in Conway.  They have 675 members that attend regularly and 25 of them are University of Central Arkansas students.  They feel that student involvement is extremely important because it helps them focus on what is truly important (Pat Johnson, employee of First United Methodist Church). 

            The purpose of the Christian Church is to be caring, accepting, compassionate, and friendly to others.  They have Christian fellowship that is committed to becoming faithful disciples.  They strive to share the good news of God’s love with others.  Some of the Christian Church goals include developing a ministry to reach non-Christian people and new residents, to continue the strong sense of fellowship, and to develop a ministry with youth and young adults (http://www.gbgm-umc.org).  The Christ Church in Conway has been established for seven years.  They have 75 members that attend regularly and three University of Central Arkansas students that attend.  They feel that student involvement is important because it gives them someone to relate to with the same beliefs.  The church tries to be involved in any way that they can with both the students and the entire community (Darren Huckey, employee of Christ Church).

            By explaining how each religion is practiced, people can better understand the church follower’s perception of life socially.  This atlas has proven that the social aspects of University of Central Arkansas students are affected by their involvement in the churches they may or may not attend.  The charts that we have included give some of the results of our student surveys concerning whether or not students practice religion and if they do which ones are most common.  The map of Conway included gives an insight on how the location of the church may affect the area and the church itself.  By looking at the map you can plainly see that most of the churches are located near busy areas of Conway.  By learning about each religion we learn more about ourselves, others, and the lifestyles that we all lead.
