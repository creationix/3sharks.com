How the populations of Conway and Little Rock Arkansas relate to each other?

Census

The city of Conway’s population is a dramatically increasing population. It ranks near the top of Arkansas in population growth. Over the past 10 years the population has jumped from 26,481 to 43,167, and it is still rising. This is an increased rate of about 63 percent over 10 years. When looking at the population pyramid in 1990 one sees that Conway experienced a population boom in the past 20 years. The chart indicates the age group 18-24 consisted of almost 6,500 people, which was 25 percent of the population. Over the following ten years these were the people who increased the population by having children. The chart shows that the group containing the ages 25-44 had a population of 7,000 in 1990 and most of them were having children. Now look at the ages 5-17, and one will see that there were 4,000; these people were added to the 25-44 populations over the next 10 years. When one looks at the 2000 census it shows a population increase to almost 47,000, which is a 63 percent increase in only 10 years. Can Conway support this increase? Some answers to this question can be found in the information above by examining the population located in the 15-35 age group. They are the ones adding to the population by primarily the natural birth rate. Natural birth rate is the difference between the number of births and the number of deaths. The main reason for the drastic increase is the fact that many people are trying to get away from the big city atmosphere that Little Rock has. This is because people want to live in an average sized town where they do not have to deal with the big city atmosphere and where they can live comfortably in a city that can suit their needs. Conway suits all of these needs quite well. Conway also is a great location for people looking to get away from Little Rock because it is only about 25 minutes away. So if a person residing in Little Rock decides that he or she wants to pack up and move to a smaller city but keep his or her same job in Little Rock, Conway is a great due to the relatively short commute.

Conway’s population is increasing rapidly, and the planning and housing commissions have to be right on top of that so they can make sure that Conway is capable of supporting that large of an increase if it continues for the next 10 years. Conway is one of the fastest growing cities in the state of Arkansas, and it has to realize that if Conway continues at this rate, it may not be able to support the increase.

Little Rock is a town that attracts many people causing the population to grow. Although there are many attractions to Little Rock the population has decreased by 0.54 percent. In 1990 the estimated population in Little Rock was 176,136. Each year through 1999 the population decreased by 0.06 percent each year. In 1999 the population of Little Rock was 177,086. Some of the causes for the decrease in population are the increase in buildings, highway roads, and other construction. Construction takes away homes and room for new homes to be built.

There are many races that make up all of Little Rock. The most dominant race of Little Rock is Caucasian.

The population is measured in different ways. It can be measured by ways of urban and rural residences, and for children through school enrollment. The total population for urban and rural residences is 175,781 as of 2000. These areas include the people that are living in houses or own some part of housing. Then the children are measured on educational reasons. Children over the age of 3 who are enrolled in school make up 45.957 percent of the population.

Little Rock is a busy place and although the population decreased over the past ten years, counties around it have been growing. Little Rock will probably grow in the future.

The relationship between the populations of Little Rock and Conway can be seen in the information above. Conway and Little Rock are only 29 miles apart so their populations mix and intertwine. Some people who live in Conway work in Little Rock and commute every day. The reason for the decrease in Little Rock’s population is because people are moving to the rural area of Conway and similar towns to get away from the city life of Little Rock. As the population of Conway increases the distance between Little Rock and Conway will decrease until they are so close together that people will not know where Little Rock begins and Conway ends.

In the end, we conclude that Little Rock and Conway’s populations are linked together. People in Conway commute back and forth from Little Rock in order to work, and Conway also serves as a haven for people who do not wish to live in a large city. The massive increase in Conway’s population shows a trend that is changing Little Rock. The larger the city grows the more people will be moving to Conway.
