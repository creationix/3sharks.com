CHURCHES IN FAULKNER COUNTY

 

            Faulkner County is located in the central part of Arkansas.  Arkansas, along with most of the other states located in the southeast portion of the United States, is predominately occupied by individuals with a Baptist and Methodist religious affiliation (Macmillan 1974. P.264).  Conway, Faulkner counties biggest city, has had incredible population growth.  The population of Conway in 1980 stood at 26,026 inhabitants.  Conway’s population in 1997 was 44,254 with an expected population of 52,167 in the year 2002.  That is a 70% increase from 1980 to 1997, and a projected increase from 1997 to the year 2002 of 18% (Community Demographics. Pop. households).  With this kind of population growth there has been a demand for more churches to be built and for churches already built, to rebuild in order to fulfill the growing membership.  For example, the Advent Lutheran Church started with 92 members in 1987 at their first site on Front Street.  The congregation continued to grow and on December 4, 1988 Advent organized as a congregation of the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, Arkansas/Oklahoma Synod.  Since 1988 the congregation has still continued to grow, this resulted in a warehouse at another location on Front Street to be remodeled in 1992 to house the church.  In May of 1995 the church began work on a new building on Farris Street which was completed October 1995.  Today 153 people claim Advent as their church and an average of 97 attending weekly worship and Sunday School (www.geocities.com).  The University Church of Christ also meets in smaller groups in members homes.   People choose these groups for their individual needs for example age, location, or family make-up (www.cyberback.com/~rblue).  Many of the people that make up the population of Conway are new residents.   Individuals that have lived there 0-5 years occupy 60% of the households in Conway.  Though this is a short time, 62% of these people own their houses which probably means they plan on staying awhile (Community Demographics. Residence).

(Community Demographics. Owners and Renters)

 The owners of houses compared to renters are probably more likely to become affiliated with a local church due to their planned length of residence.  These circumstances only add to the overall church population of Conway and Faulkner County.

With this information in mind, we start to understand the answer to the question at hand; what are the reasons and circumstances behind the spatial distribution of churches in Faulkner County?  We know from the information already given that population growth in Faulkner County has been and will continue to be a big factor.  But there are so many other factors that need to be included to fully answer the question.  Things not as obvious as population such as age groups, education, occupation, income and wealth, commuting, race and origin, and most importantly religious preferences all fit into the reasons behind distribution of churches.  All of these things put together will give a good idea of why and where certain churches are located in Faulkner County. Religion may intimately affect all facets of a culture. Formalized and organized religion is an institutional expression of the sociological subsystem.

The Catholic church is the largest and oldest church by far.  The church has been around for almost two thousand years and it contains one billion members, nearly on sixth the population of the world.  One thing that sets the Catholic church apart from others is that they believe one is saved though works alone.  Catholics believe that baptism, the Eucharist, penance, confirmation, holy orders, matrimony, and the anointing of the sick, collectively called the seven sacraments must be performed with exception of matrimony, holy orders and anointing of the sick in order to obtain salvation.  The Catholics also believe that the pope is of direct descent from Jesus’ disciple Peter.  The Catholic religion is the most complicated Christian religion because of its many rules and doctrines (www.catholic.com).  Baptists believe that one can achieve salvation by believing in Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior.  Baptists must be spiritually reborn as symbolized through their baptism.  Baptists believe that the Bible has the final say in all matters regarding faith and practices.  By studying, memorizing, and practicing what is written in the Bible the Baptists believe that one may have a full and fruitful life (www.lvbaptist.org).  The Lutheran church is a combination of the Baptist and Catholic churches.  The Lutherans believe in the Trinity of God and that the Trinity died on the cross and rose again three days later.  Lutherans believe that God gives forgiveness and everlasting life to those who believe in Jesus Christ.  The Lutherans church teaches that the Holy Spirit was sent to us to strengthen us.  Lutherans also believe that the Bible was inspired by God and that it is the authority for our daily living (www.stlukesfedway.org). The Christian faith as a whole is based on the same teachings of Jesus and the Bible.  Each denomination however has their own different way of interpreting these teachings.  These interpretations are what make these denominations so different.  The Catholic Church quotes Bible verses that say how a Christian is supposed to worship and have fellowship with God while the Baptist, Methodist, and Lutheran denominations all quote different verses directly from the Bible that say how one is supposed to worship and have fellowship with God which are completely different from the Catholics’ verses yet from the same Bible.

            The median age of individuals living in Faulkner County is up 6.5 years from 1980 to 1997 with the largest age group consisting of 35-49 year olds.  This group makes up 21% of the population (Community Demographics. Age groups).   

(Community Demographics. Age groups)

 When our group interviewed various churches in Faulkner County we found that most reported their membership was made up of predominately young adults 20-40 years of age.  Reason being the city of Conway contains three colleges.  “The strength of our church is our young couples.  They’re so dynamic.” (Worshipping in Our Community, pg. 4, Johnnie Hoffmann).  By recognizing the fact that 18-24 year olds make up roughly 17% (second biggest group) of households by age (Community Demographics. Age groups) one can assume that this is the reason the younger age group makes up a large percent of churchgoers.  Also, the boom of families moving to Conway in the 80’s helped with this figure.  When the families moved here in the 80’s with young children, it was likely that their children would grow up and purchase their own residence in Conway.  These circumstances have made Conway into a young populated community.  With this in mind, churches in Faulkner County are going to be changing in various ways to accommodate these young adults.

            By studying the commuting time of individuals churches can come up with the best possible meeting times for church activities (Community Demographics. Commute to work).

(Community Demographics. Commute to work)

 Median commute is 18 minutes for residents of Faulkner County, churches can take this information and plan the time of their services taking into consideration miscellaneous time spent in between commuting time for changing clothes and dinner that would allow time to make it to church.  Of the churches we interviewed, the percentage of their church members that traveled more than 10 miles ranged from 1% to 70%.  Though most of these percentages were just guesses, it does show that the location of the church really does not affect the membership.  But by knowing the percentage of members who travel 10 miles or more, churches can schedule services at a convenient time for their current members.  Commuting can also affect the choice of the church’s location if they were to rebuild.  If a church knew that most of their membership was traveling from the same region, they could look to rebuild closer to that area.

            Race and Ethnicity play a huge part in what denominations are represented in Faulkner County.  By looking at graphs composed in the Community Demographics chart on race and ethnicity, we see that the white population makes up an enormous part of the population of Faulkner County with 88%.

(Community Demographics. Race and ethnicity)

 It also shows that white is the fastest growing racial group in 1997 at 3% per year.  Certain races prefer certain denominations.  Therefore if you have an area like Faulkner County that is not representative of the Indian civilization, you will not have a lot of Hinduism which is the predominant religion in India.  But with a predominant Western European, Protestant churches will be abundant.  So with very little representation of ethnic groups like Asian, Native, or Hispanic in Faulkner County, you can not expect many churches characteristic of their religion.

            The most important thing that affects the distribution of churches in Faulkner County is religious preferences.  As was noted at the beginning, Arkansas is located in the part of the United States that is largely Baptist or Methodist.  Faulkner County is characteristic of this with Southern Baptists Convention containing 13,140 adherents in 1990.  That was 22% of the total county population at the time.  Methodist came in second with roughly 7% of Faulkner County’s population (Denominational preferences). (Community Demographics.  Denominational preferences)

Among the major religious groups in Faulkner County, Protestants make up 56% of the

population, Catholic about 7%, Jewish only 0.02%, 38% of the population do not attend any type of church. The number of churches for each religious group, for the most part,

corresponds with the number of adherents in Faulkner County area.  There are 30 Southern Baptist convention churches spread throughout Faulkner County, the most among churches.  The United Methodist church, which contains the second largest number of adherents, has 15 churches in Faulkner County.  Catholics have 1 church, and Jewish 0 in the county (Religious Preferences).

            In Faulkner County it is safe to say that a lot of the distribution of churches is due to population growth.  As you read in the interviews we conducted, roughly all of the churches said that they have moved locations or added on to the present location due to growth.  Upon their move, they are looking for a place that will be convenient to their members.  With the continued growth of Faulkner County, you will see many more churches popping up in Faulkner County containing young adults.  The older members of the County will probably continue to go to their present church, while the new residents to Conway will probably look for the most convenient church for them to reach that is their denomination.
