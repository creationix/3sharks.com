Conway’s Water Supply

 

     Where does our water come from?  The answer would be the Brewer Lake.  Conway has two sewage

 

system.  We need two sewer system because we’re holding 28,000 Conway residents, but UCA doesn’t

 

count.  The reason is because majority of UCA students do not live in Conway.  Some students live in

 

Little Rock, Vilonia, Greenbrier, Morrilton, and etc.  There’s actually more people in Conway, but not all

 

are residents.

 

     I did an interview with John Wofford who works at Conway Corp.  An interview with him is very

 

interesting.First,we talked about who owns the water supply.  The city of Conway district owns the water

 

supply.  The water supply is a nonprofit organization.  The government of Arkansas really owns the supply

 

for the whole state.

 

     Then we talked about going through a drought.  Conway goes through a 50 years drought.  The record

 

(McGoodwin, Williams and Yates, inc.) shows that every 50 years we loose water that the average weight

 

of the water.  It started in 1853, again in 1901, and again in 1954.Now we are going through a drought. 

 

Our lake is like a recharge.  Every year, we need lots of rain to replace the loss of water, like recharge, in

 

the Brewer Lake.  Last year, we were short by four percent.  This year, we are short by eleven percent. 

 

Before the summer hits, we need to go back to 100 percent of our water supply.  Our most rain fall is

 

between March and April.  Last year since we were behind by four percent, people of Conway had to take

 

turns to water their lawns.  Here’s an example; Monday, Wednesday, and Friday are the odd numbers of

 

houses that have the odd house numbers can water their lawn.  Then, Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday the

 

even numbers of the house numbers can water their lawn.  This year we behind in water by eleven percent

 

and April is already over.  This is how our drought works.  It may take seven tears or so for our water

 

supply to be back to normal.  I asked Mr. Wofford if the global warming could have any effect towards our

 

water supply.  He didn’t understand what global warming is and I had to explain it to him.  Then he said

 

maybe  have an effect with the drought, but he don’t believe that global warming have anything to do with

 

our water supply.  I said “okay”.  Then he said, “we will never run out of water, because there’s other lakes

 

around here to replace our lake.”

 

     I asked him, “ what if Conway overpopulates?”  He laughed and said, “what do you mean over-

 

populate?    I told him that what if by year 2020, the population rise to 100,000 residents.  Will we run out

 

of water then?  He said that we’ll never run out of water.  Right now, we have 28,000 residents and the

 

McGoodwin, Williams, and Yates did some analysis the population range at an average annual

 

compounding growth rate of 3.01 percent and 3.92 percent.  So, this could mean that by the year 2030, the

 

population growth would be 120,000 residents.  McGoodwin, Williams, and Yates engineers wants to make

 

a projects to get more water supply to Conway by the year 2030.  They want to use Geers ferry Lake to be

 

our water supply.

 

     Monthly water sales for the period of January 1993 through December 1997 have gone up.  In 1998 to

 

1999, water supply consumption had decrease because of the drought.  Rich people uses more water than

 

poor people, rich people probably uses more water because watering grass or filling up their swimming

 

pool.  In 1993, 50 percent of the water supply used by the residents.  Twenty percent is used by the

 

industrial, and commercial uses sixteen percent.  The institutional uses eight percent, and municipal uses

 

one percent.  Each year it goes up by ten percent.  They (McGoodwin, Williams and Yates, Inc,) predicts

 

that by the year 2030, the average water usage will be about 27 millions to 45 millions of gallons of water. 

 

Our lake holds about 60 to 90 millions of gallons, by Mr. Wofford.

 

      I asked Mr. Wofford about the underground gas tanks have any effect to our water supply.  He said,

 

“no, only the well waters (underground water) would have the higher risks”.  Mr. Wofford believes that we

 

don’t have any problem with underground gas tanks leak.
