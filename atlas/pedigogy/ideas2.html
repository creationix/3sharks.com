P E D I G O G Y

Atlas #2 Project Ideas

Atlas Project Ideas: Some Ideas to Get You Started, Not an Inclusive List!

Updated: 3/5/2000

Atlas #2, Human Geography and/or Mapping


B. HUMAN GEOGRAPHY:


B.1. Demographics (fertility, mortality and migration)

1. Where do the freshmen of UCA come from? In-state, and where? Out-of-state, and where. Foreign, and where? Are there clear in-state migration streams? Is UCA predominately in-state students? What role do you think the difference in in-state versus out-of-state tuition might play?

2. Conway is booming! Exploding at the seams! Why? Is it from births or migration? If migration, then from where, and how much? If current migration or fertility trends continue, what will Conway’s population be like?

3. Death. Everybody does it. But what do people in this state die from? Have the major causes of death changed over time? How? Are there major differences in cause of death by age, sex, income or race? Is there a relationship between urbanization and rates of death?

4. Is there a correlation between level of economic development (incomes) and fertility rates, i.e., as incomes rise the number of children per family drops, or vice versa?

5. What do the population pyramids look like for Conway versus other cities and the state. What can you tell about the current population e.g. their age makeup, sex ratios, future fertility, and what predictions can you make, e.g. future demands for schools, nursing homes, etc?


B.2. Cultural Attributes

1. Why don’t students bicycle on campus? Why don’t students bicycle to campus? Is it a cultural attitude (Arkansans don’t cycle) or are most students commuters? Is it because of the lack of bike lanes and bike racks on campus? Is there a pent up demand for a safe way to bike to campus?

2. Assess the changing role of men, women and children over the early history of the state by selecting an old cemetery. Map the location and size of headstones by sex and age at death. Examine, describe and map the differences.

3. Have churches physically migrated over time in Conway or Little Rock. Have churches physically moved from the center city to be closer to their members in the suburbs? Or, have new churches broken off from existing churches to serve those communities? Or do members simply commute longer distances to the church?

4. What is the religious affiliation of people in Conway or Faulkner County, or Little Rock? Which religion/denomination/sect is the oldest? Which is the biggest? Are there ethnic differences? Are their race differences? Do any Native American Indians sects remain?

5. Trace the migration of blues music, or folk, or gospel. Where did it start? How did it get here? What kind of diffusion process played a role?


B.3. Spatial Behavior

1. The Geography of Football: From where has UCA recruited its football players for the last four years. Are there distinct migration streams. Do the coaches have personal contacts with these high schools?

2. What spatial barriers exist within 4 blocks of the UCA campus that would discourage alternative transportation (walking, cycling) e.g., lack of sidewalks, open ditches, lack of designated bike lanes, curb-cuts, traffic signals, etc. What is the condition of sidewalks near UCA? Over the last couple years, how and why have UCA parking lots migrated? Does UCA provide shuttle service or a way to get from these satellite parking lots? Are there personal safety issues in walking from campus to satellite parking lots at night?

3. Is the UCA campus in full compliance with the Disabilities Act? Is the entire campus accessible to the disabled?

4. What are the patterns concerning signs on UCA’s campus? Do all buildings have easily identifiable signs on the exterior? As a newcomer, how easy is it to navigate around the campus? Could you find specific buildings, specific room numbers?

5. Where are most of the fire alarm calls on campus? How often must they be tested? How often are they pulled unnecessarily? Are both types spatially clustered in a particular type of building? Is there a temporal pattern, i.e. more likely during early evening? Is there a seasonal pattern, i.e. more likely during mid-terms and finals?

6. Is UCA the “campus that took over Conway?” Map out the parcels that UCA owns (you may be VERY surprised to see how much UCA owns!) around the campus. Compare it to the “UCA Master Development Plan.” Is all that land necessary? What will it be used for? Was it expensive to obtain? Did some alumni make a killing (wink, wink, nudge, nudge)? All of this is public record, it will just take some digging down at City Hall, the UCA administration office and the physical plant.


B.4. Political Geography

1. What impact could UCA students have on local Conway elections? Is the student body of a sufficient size? Given the low registration and turnout of voters, could a fully registered and voting UCA block take over Conway’s City Council?

2. What are the various political boundaries in Conway (e.g. voting districts, city limits, water management districts, planning districts, sewer bond districts, pollution control districts, school districts) and how do they overlap? What do all these districts do, what are their duties, responsibilities, and what do they actually visibly do?

3. Based on election records, has Conway, or the state of Arkansas become more conservative or liberal over time? Is there a split between national and local elections ( e.g. voting for liberal candidates locally, conservative candidates nationally)?


B.5. Economic Geography

1. What are the major industries in Conway? Who are the major employers, and what do they contribute to the city (taxes, pollution, traffic congestion, increased commuting or permanent migration)? Do most employees move to or already live in Conway? What are the major occupations in Conway?

2. As a major employer, what is UCA’s contribution to the City of Conway, as measured in taxes (is UCA tax exempt?), pollution, traffic congestion, commuting, permanent migration)? Do most employees move to or already live in Conway? What are the major occupations employed by UCA?

3. As a major magnet for temporary residents, i.e. students, what is UCA’s contribution to the City of Conway, as measured in pollution, traffic congestion (does every student have a car?), commuting (how many student commute to campus?), permanent migration? In the 2000 U.S. Census will UCA students be counted as residing in Conway, or at their parent’s home. Is the UCA student population large enough to make a difference?

4. A “high-order good” such as cars, computers or luxury items needs a large population to support its sale. A “low-order good”, jeans, food, paper products, only needs a small population to support it. What is the population threshold for some goods and services, and how has that changed since 1985 or 1990. In other works, over the last five or 10 years how has the ratio of a particular good or store changed to population in that city. For example, Little Rock had how many computer stores, per thousand population in 1990 versus today? How much has that ratio increased. What about other goods, staples like supermarkets, convenience stores, furniture stores, auto dealerships, health clubs? Have “Big Boxes” e.g. Home Depot, Office Depot, Walmart, Best Buy, etc. changed the relationship? Have their spatial relationship changed, i.e. their locations from center city to suburbs? Note: Access to old phone book yellow pages would probably be necessary for this project, either on CD on hard copy.

5. Map the diffusion of Wal-Mart from its humble beginnings to a nationwide chain. Is it hierarchical or contagious spatial diffusion? How has Wal-Mart diffused in Arkansas? What effect has the introduction of a Walmart to a city had on local businesses?


B.6. Natural Resource Geography

1. Check out the Green Map System at http://www.greenmap.com/cities.html. How could we do something similar for Conway?

2. Given that the levy breaks, what portion of Conway would be underwater during a 100 year flood. What would be the depth of the water in some of the old and especially new subdivisions? Would UCA be underwater?

3. According to soil maps, what portion of Conway is built on soil that is deemed unsuitable for urbanization due to its poor drainage? What has Conway done to address the problems of drainage in the city? What is Conway doing to prevent future drainage problems as the city grows?

4. Where are hazardous materials used, stored, dumped and disposed of on campus? Are we in any danger from their release, or their old storage? Ditto for Conway. What is their relationship to the aquifer, or to the storm drainage system? Could they get into either of those?


B.7. Urban Geography

1. How are expanding cities (Conway, Little Rock, etc.) planning to handle future growth? Do they have plans in place for new schools, roads, sewers, etc., and how will these services be paid for?

2. How have land-uses in Conway changed over time? Can you predict future land use based on existing land use? How strict are land use regulations, anyway? How does land use relate to zoning?

3. How and why have the city limits of Conway changed over time? What is involved in being annexed by the City, and after annexation, what services must the city provide. Do land owners or developers pay the city (to defer costs) to be annexed? Can the City refuse annexation? In what direction is the city growing?


B.8. Regional Geography

1. Collect and produce an area profile of the Conway area (city and hinterland). Include population, sources of growth, projected population, description of flora, fauna, government and society. How would you fashion a packet designed to lure new businesses, residents here?

2. Describe the cultural and economic region controlled by Little Rock. What surrounding cities feed into it? How does Little Rock relate to these other smaller cities? For cultural attributes, are most radio stations located in Little Rock, are the better restaurants located in Little Rock, are upscale clothing stores located in Little Rock? For economic attributes, are most stores and businesses supplied from Little Rock, do service customers go to or come from Little Rock?


B.9. Socially Constructed Geography

1. How many gated communities can be found in Conway? What is their purpose? Are gated communities (ranging from just a fence or wall to an actual gate and guard) new in Conway? Are they becoming the norm? Who do the gates exclude?

2. Describe and map social control districts, i.e. places where behavior is controlled. These could include; malls, public parks, university campuses, gated communities, public meeting places, public streets. Where are these social control districts, who are they trying to control, and why are they trying to control them?


C. GEOGRAPHIC INFORMATION SYSTEMS (GIS):

1. What is the availability of maps and blueprints for the UCA campus? What is available, in what form, for what period to time, etc?

2. What is the availability of maps for the City of Conway? What is available, in what form, for what period to time, etc?

3. Map the transportation systems in Conway, interstate, primary and secondary roads. Have Conway’s commercial growth shifted toward the interstate? Where has Conway’s residential growth shifted?
