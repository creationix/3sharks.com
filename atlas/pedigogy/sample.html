Sample Project & Instructions

A9: Physical Geography: Floods

“Floods and Fauna in Arkansas”

By Wiley Writer, Paul Proofreader and Casey Riback (Whole Lotta Flooding Going On)

Principles of Geography: Geog 1305, Section 2619

Prepared: March 3, 2000


How Have Annual Floods Effected the Land forms of Arkansas and How Have Humans Tried To Control Floods?


The text is to be submitted in 12 point type, Times New Roman font, with paper margins of one inch on all sides and double spaced. For the draft, one printed copy will be submitted for review. For the final submission, two hard (printed) copies must be submitted as well as one electronic copy (on disk). The disk must have a label indicating the group name, title, assignment, date and group member names. Remember to staple your report. All electronic submission must be in PC Windows Microsoft Word and/or Excel and/or ArcView.

Pay particular attention to the citations (see “Guide to citing sources for Geography 1305"in your syllabus). The intent is to take these research projects and place them on the UCA Geography department’s web page. In other words, once published, anyone will be able to view these reports. So, be careful about citing your sources. By the same token, do not make your report a lengthy string of quotes. No more than 20 words should be used in a single quote. Paraphrase when necessary, put the material in your own words as much as possible. The Writing Center has a number of handouts on how to cite materials. Please note that if you are directly citing a web site in your text that you should provide the abbreviated link in the text (e.g., http:\www.theatlantic.com) and the full citation including title in your reference section. Internet web site citations should give the reader information on who is sponsoring or writing the material. If you are attaching maps or illustrations to your report, they should be referenced and discussed in the text. Do not let them simply hang off the back of the report.

Every word processing program has a spell check feature. Use it. Drafts and finals submitted with misspelled words will be given a zero (1/5 of your grade). At the very minimum have at least one other person proof and edit your work before you submit it. I will mark down for poor grammar, organization, etc. Also note that both the draft and the final must be reviewed by the Writing Center and a Center slip must be attached to the draft or final. For the final submission, the page from the draft with my comments must also be submitted.
